Microsoft HoloLens

Microsoft HoloLens are a pair of mixed reality smartglasses developed and manufactured by Microsoft. HoloLens was the first head-mounted display running the Windows Mixed Reality platform under the Windows 10 computer operating system. The glasses come with a 2k see-through screen which can display software such as a Holographic version of the Windows operating system, 3D Model Viewer, and Microsoft Edge. It has 3GB of RAM and 64GB of storage, and is controlled through hand gestures. There are currently 342 apps compatible with HoloLens, ranging from games to langauge learning tools.



Daily Life

The only place to buy Microsoft HoloLens is on the Microsoft store, and they come at a whopping $4,749 CAD. Due to this large price, the average person will never use one in their daily lives. However if they were to, they could display 3D objects in their home using augmented reality, watch videos, play a variety of games, and even use Skype to video call.



Education

Microsoft's Mixed Reality platform offers many opportunities for education. It supports applications that show anatomy, tours of places throughout history and space, chemisty, and more, all using Augmented Reality.



Medical

HoloLens assists with Medical Procedures by showing real-time visuals of the patient, instead of referring to another screen. With HoloLens that information can basically be displayed right infront of you, as if it was part of your vision.



Video Games

Out of the few hundered HoloLens apps on the Microsoft Store, quite a few of them are games. Some of them are simple and display some 3D object in your home, and some are more complex such as 2-player games, puzzles, and even virtual characters. Some pre-existing games even have Mixed Reality support, such as Minecraft. I was surprised by how many games there are, and I think some of them look quite interesting and fun.



The Internet

Mozilla released a version of FireFox compatible with HoloLens. It works like regular web browser and also showcases the capabilities of HoloLens to web developers.
